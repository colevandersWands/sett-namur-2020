inclure l'informatique

7 conseils


(début)

1.

vous risquez plus de mal que du bien

  • "no" can be the right answer
  • be aware of existing stereotypes & expectations
  • first exposures to programming can be difficult to overcome

2.

s'amuser c'est clé

  1. your goal should be to deliver confidence and relevance, not specific skills
  2. prioritize positive experiences over any explicit outcomes
  3. fit it as smoothly as possible into normal flows
  4. very very little could be enough

3.

l'informatique dans ta matière,
où est-elle déjà?

start with good analytical strategy
computing should begin when the questions are clear and answerable
where does it belong in your discipline?

4.

continuez à enseigner ta matière

  • include informatics as it's actually used in your discipline. the tools, the methods,..
  • and sometimes this may mean "no"
  • you're not a programmer, students aren't there to become one

5.

d'abord le contexte, puis le code

  • if you're not sure how programming fits in, they won't either
  • "computational thinking" is not some magical free-floating thing
  • its grounded in domains, techniques, and technologies
  • introduce these practices first to understand why

6.

l'informatique:
une partenaire d'enquête

make it clear this isn't a tool students fall back on for answers like with google
it's a partner in asking questions, the answers must come from them
in interpreting and confidencing the results

7.

"flip the script"

do your students already have a programming class or activity?
maybe it's best to integrate your subject into there, instead of the other way around
